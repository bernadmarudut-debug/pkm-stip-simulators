Maritime English Communication

What is Maritime English?

Maritime English is the specialized English used in the shipping industry. IMO recognizes it as the official working language at sea. It covers navigation, operations, safety, and social communication.

Maritime Numbers

Course/Bearing: Spoken digit by digit. 045 = "Zero Four Five"

Time: 4-digit UTC. 0830 = "Zero Eight Three Zero UTC"

Position: Lat 06°10'S = "Latitude Zero Six degrees One Zero minutes South"

SMCP Message Markers

Standard phrases that begin every communication:

INSTRUCTION
"INSTRUCTION. Do not overtake."
ADVICE
"ADVICE. Alter course to port."
WARNING
"WARNING. Dangerous area ahead."
INFORMATION
"INFORMATION. Pilot boarding at 0800."

Other markers: QUESTION, ANSWER, REQUEST, INTENTION

VHF Calling Procedure

Ship:Jakarta Port Control (x3). This is MV Bintang Laut (x3). Call sign: Bravo India November Tango. Over.
Port:MV Bintang Laut, this is Jakarta Port Control. Go ahead. Over.

Rules: Name called 3x, own name 3x. "Over" = expecting reply. "Out" = end. Never "Over and Out"!

Standard Helm Orders

Port ten → Turn 10° left

Starboard twenty → Turn 20° right

Midships → Rudder amidships

Hard port/starboard → Maximum rudder

Steady → Maintain current heading

Steer [course] → Head to specific course

Critical: Helmsman MUST repeat back every order before executing!

Three Levels of Emergency

MAYDAY (Distress)

Grave and imminent danger. Life-threatening situation. Requires immediate assistance.

PAN PAN (Urgency)

Urgent situation but no immediate danger to life. Engine failure, medical emergency.

SECURITE (Safety)

Navigation safety information. Weather warnings, floating objects, unlit vessels.

MAYDAY Call Format

Call:MAYDAY MAYDAY MAYDAY. This is MV Nusantara (x3). MAYDAY MV Nusantara. Position: Lat 06°30'S, Long 110°20'E. I am on fire. 25 POB. Require firefighting assistance. Over.

Man Overboard

Immediate: Shout "MAN OVERBOARD, PORT/STARBOARD!" → Throw lifebuoy → Press MOB → Alert bridge

Bridge: 3 prolonged blasts → Execute turn → Broadcast PAN PAN on Ch 16

Port Arrival Communication

Ship:Port Control, this is MV Samudra. INFORMATION. Approaching pilot station. ETA 1300 UTC. REQUEST. Pilot and berth. Over.
Port:MV Samudra. Berth 107, Terminal 3. Pilot at 1300. Reduce to 6 knots. Over.

Pilotage Communication

Pilot:Good morning Captain. Ship's draft?
Captain:Forward 8.5m, aft 9.2m. Course 180, speed 8 knots.
Pilot:I will take the con. Starboard ten.
Helm:Starboard ten, Sir.

Berthing Commands

Tugs: "Make fast forward/aft." "Push/Pull." "Let go."

Lines: "Send heaving line." "Make fast head/stern/spring line." "All fast fore and aft."

Engine: "Dead slow ahead." "Slow ahead." "Half ahead." "Full ahead." "Stop engine." "Dead slow astern."
